PRIORY OF THE ORANGE TREE MAP

Becca Halter

Welcome to the world mapped in the style of "The Prioriy of the Orange Tree" and "A Day of Fallen Night" by Samantha Shannon. This page takes you through different aspects of the style and how I brought it to our reality.

While I was tempted to use some of the bold coloring on the covers of the books, the maps themselves are simple, but intricate black and white line work, which is the classic hand-drawn fantasy style.

I used an off white to mimic the slight coloring of a book page. Adding the shading to the outer coastline and a wave texture, highlights isolated landforms. You can see this clearly see the coastline shadow effect looking at the island of Japan.

Looking at the Mediterranen Sea you can see how major geographic features like, mountains and deserts are represented. These different habitats are often important to developing the scene of a novel and also frequently serve as plot points of a novel.

Here, along the western coast of Africa you can see the boundaries that would represent different political entities that underly the intrigue in most fantasy novels.

Looking at Mexico, note how a unique icon marks key settlements. Indentifying where these cities are is critical to understanding the setting of a fantasy novel.

The Priory style at a continental scale.

An image of the central New York showing how parks are represented by a grove of trees and other points of interest by a small house. Other evidence of human geogrpahy is absent at this scale.

At this larger scale of Albany, NY, given the Priory map is only regional, I took the liberty of adding in roads as a key mode of travel. Characters often use routes like these in the course of the story.

Looking more closely at downtown Albany, I also added in pedestrain walkways using a dotted line style. Other features are limited to points of interest.
